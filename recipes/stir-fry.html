A simple, humble, tofu stir-fry recipe.

This dish does not overpromise. It doesn't claim to be authentic asian cuisine or particularly complex in flavor. What it does promise is tasty, quick, healthy, protein-packed satisfaction to get you through the day when all you can really do is the bare minimum. The tofu stir-fry doesn't ask much of us and that's why we love it.

Ingredients

  • Extra Firm Tofu
  • Broccoli cut into bite sized pieces
  • Other veggie of your choice, carrots, bellpeppers, and mushrooms are great
  • Soy Sauce
  • Maple Syrups, agave syrup, or your preferred sweetener
  • Sesame Oil
  • Rice Vinegar
  • Chili Flakes if you like spicys
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Sesame Seeds (Optional mostly for increased aesthetics)

Steps

  1. Slice tofu into cubes and press for however long you have time for
  2. Preheat oven to 400
  3. Get a large mixing bowl and add some corn starch, garlic powder, and salt and pepper to your liking
  4. After tofu has pressed for at least 10 minutes, mix tofu into the corn starch mix until tofu is coated
  5. Put parchment paper on a baking sheet and place tofu cubes on sheet.
  6. Put tofu into the oven and bake for 20-30 minutes
  7. Heat up a pan on medium heat with your preferred oil that can stand up to hotter temps, avocado oil is good.
  8. Slice onion and add to pan
  9. After about 2 mins, or when onion starts to soften, add the rest of your veggies after you've cut them up into bite sized pieces
  10. Turn heat up to medium high if needed
  11. While veggies cook, mix soy sauce, sweetener, rice vinegar together in a cup. This recipe is not particular enough to give you exact amounts, just do what feels right.
  12. Mince garlic and add to veggies as soon they have softened
  13. Let garlic cook for a minute or so, then add the sauce to your veggie mix
  14. Add baked tofu cubes to stir fry and combine
  15. Add additional soy sauce if needed and top with chili flakes and sesame seeds
